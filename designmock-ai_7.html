The problem

Why talented designers fail interviews

The gap between being a great designer and interviewing like one is real. Here's what most candidates get wrong.

Common mistake
📖

Weak storytelling

Designers describe what they did, not why decisions were made or what problems they were solving. Recruiters want narrative, not feature lists.

Common mistake
📊

No metrics discussion

Vague outcomes like "improved user experience" don't land. Interviewers at top companies expect conversion rates, NPS changes, and retention data.

Common mistake
💡

Shallow product thinking

Answering design questions without demonstrating business context, user research strategy, or tradeoff reasoning signals junior-level thinking.
